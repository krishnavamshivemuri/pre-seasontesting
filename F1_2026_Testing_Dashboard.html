üí° KEY INSIGHTS & ANALYSIS

üèÜ How to Interpret This Data

Testing ‚â† Reality: Pre-season testing data should be taken with a grain of salt. Teams often run different programs - some focus on reliability, others on pure pace, and many hide their true performance. However, certain patterns are hard to disguise.

What to Look For:

  • Consistency: Teams with low standard deviation are typically more stable and easier to develop throughout the season.
  • Improvement Rate: Teams showing significant improvement from Day 1 to Day 3 demonstrate they're learning quickly and extracting performance.
  • Sector Balance: Teams strong across all three sectors tend to be more competitive in varied circuit conditions.
  • Top Speed vs Lap Time: High top speed with slower lap times suggests drag issues; slow top speed with fast lap times indicates excellent downforce.
  • Lap Volume: More laps = better reliability testing, which is crucial for race performance.
